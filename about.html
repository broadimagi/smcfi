Who We Are

Rooted in Taguig, built for youth transformation

The Simbayanan ni Maria Community Foundation, Inc. (SMCFI) is a non-stock, non-profit, and PCNC-accredited organization dedicated to providing educational opportunities to underprivileged yet deserving youth in Taguig City.

Since its establishment in 1991, SMCFI has supported thousands of scholars and produced more than 500 graduates who now contribute to their communities and professions.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

To provide holistic formation on servant leadership; academic excellence; and leadership engagement in the community for the poor but deserving youth in the Community of Taguig and its nearby cities and municipalities.

Vision

A community development foundation in Taguig City that provides social empowerment to the poor youth as servants and future leaders.

Objectives

  1. To provide education by means of formal and non-formal scholarships, trainings and seminars to the people of Taguig, particularly the youth of Taguig and its nearby cities and municipalities.
  2. To undertake special projects for the community in times of crisis and fortuitous events.
  3. To enhance the social responsibility of the people of Taguig and its nearby cities and municipalities by advocating community service and social services.
  4. To consolidate the resources of the city of Taguig and its nearby cities and municipalities, particularly by means of donations, grants and fund raising projects, to help the poor and marginalized sector of the community.
  5. To network with other institutions, whether non-government and government, local and abroad, particularly the Simbayanan ni Maria Multi-Purpose Cooperative (SMMPC), and to skill-up socio-economic projects for the benefit of Taguig City and its nearby cities and municipalities.
  6. To undertake values formation, leadership engagement, academic excellence, and to strengthen the principles and practices of community building, servant leadership, sustainable development, and the spirit of cooperativism.

Our History

SMCFI began with educational and livelihood assistance for Taguig parishioners. Across more than three decades, that early service grew into a community foundation dedicated to youth formation, scholarship support, and social empowerment.

1989

The first act of service

Parish priest Fr. Anton Pascual provided educational and livelihood assistance to Taguig parishioners.

1991

SMCFI was established

With the support of generous individuals, SMMPC and SMCFI were established. SMCFI became a non-stock, non-profit organization on March 20, 1991.

1996

Recognized by a national NGO network

SMCFI was accredited by the Association of Foundations, the largest network of NGOs in the Philippines.

2005

A dedicated community foundation

On May 21, 2005, SMCFI transitioned into a dedicated community foundation focused on youth transformation.

2008

DSWD registration and license

On August 15, 2008, SMCFI received Registration and License to Operate with DSWD as a community-based social welfare agency.

2013

Civil society accreditation

SMCFI was accredited as a civil society organization by the Sangguniang Panlungsod of Taguig City Government.

2017

PCNC accreditation

SMCFI received accreditation from the Philippine Council for NGO Certification (PCNC) on May 25, 2017.

2019

Scholarship program accreditation

SMCFI received DSWD Accreditation for the Simbayanan ni Maria Scholarship Program.

2022

Renewed DSWD Accreditation

SMCFI renewed its DSWD Accreditation at Level 2 with 5-year validity until 2027.

2025

Renewals and youth organization recognition

SMCFI renewed its DSWD Certificate of Registration and License to Operate, renewed its PCNC Accreditation, received accreditation for SMCFI-Samahan ng mga Iskolar ng Simbayanan as a youth organization of Taguig, and renewed its BIR Tax Exemption.

2026

35 years of service

SMCFI renewed its registration as a Donee Institution and celebrates 35 years of loyalty and impactful community service in Taguig.
