The SMCFI Story

From parish-based assistance to 35 years of community service in Taguig

SMCFI began with a practical response to the needs of Taguig parishioners: education, livelihood, and formation. Over time, that response became a community foundation focused on helping poor but deserving youth grow as servant leaders.

Beginning

Assistance With Purpose

In 1989, Fr. Anton Pascual provided educational and livelihood assistance to Taguig parishioners, planting the first seeds of what would become SMCFI.

Formation

A Foundation Takes Shape

With the support of generous individuals, SMCFI was formally established as a non-stock, non-profit organization on March 20, 1991.

Growth

Service That Endures

Through accreditations, partnerships, and scholarship work, SMCFI has continued strengthening its commitment to Taguig youth and community development.

Milestones Through the Years

1989

The first act of service

Parish priest Fr. Anton Pascual provided educational and livelihood assistance to Taguig parishioners.

1991

SMCFI was established

With the support of generous individuals, SMMPC and SMCFI were established. SMCFI became a non-stock, non-profit organization on March 20, 1991.

1996

Recognized by a national network

SMCFI was accredited by the Association of Foundations, the largest network of NGOs in the Philippines.

2005

A clearer community foundation role

On May 21, 2005, SMCFI transitioned into a dedicated community foundation focused on youth transformation.

2008

DSWD registration and license

On August 15, 2008, SMCFI received Registration and License to Operate with DSWD as a community-based social welfare agency.

2013

Civil society accreditation

SMCFI was accredited as a civil society organization by the Sangguniang Panlungsod of Taguig City Government.

2017

PCNC accreditation

SMCFI received accreditation from the Philippine Council for NGO Certification (PCNC) on May 25, 2017.

2019

Scholarship program accreditation

SMCFI received DSWD Accreditation for the Simbayanan ni Maria Scholarship Program.

2022

Renewed DSWD Accreditation

SMCFI renewed its DSWD Accreditation at Level 2 with 5-year validity until 2027.

2025

A year of renewals and recognition

SMCFI renewed its DSWD Certificate of Registration and License to Operate with 3-year validity, renewed its PCNC Accreditation with 5-year validity, received accreditation for SMCFI-Samahan ng mga Iskolar ng Simbayanan as a youth organization of Taguig, and renewed its BIR Tax Exemption.

2026

35 years of community service

SMCFI renewed its registration as a Donee Institution and celebrates 35 years of loyalty and impactful community service in Taguig.

Continue the Mission

Every milestone points to the same purpose: forming youth who can serve, lead, and help build stronger communities.
